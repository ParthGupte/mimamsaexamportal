How to use the portal




  • When logging in for the first time, use your full email ID and your generated password (in all caps). You'll be prompted to change your password the first time you log in. Your credentials, once saved, will not be reset for future tests. That means the generated password won't work anymore and you'll have to remember your new password.

  • Only one device can be logged into an account at a time. Logging in on a second device logs you out of the first.

  • On the actual exam, timers counting down to the end of the test are located on the top of the screen (for laptops and other widescreen devices) and in the side pane - which you can open by clicking on the hamburger icon - on the top right (for all devices including mobile phones). The last twenty minutes of the exam is to be used for uploading answers. This is reflected in the format in which the timer is displayed. For a 2 hour test for example, the timer counts down from "1:40:00 + 00:20:00" down to "0 seconds + 20:00" and then it switches to "19:59 (Uploading time)" and counts down from there.

  • To navigate through the paper, links for all the questions are listed on the navigation bar.

  • You are free to leave the exam at any moment. If the exam hasn't ended, you won't be barred from entering again had you left at any point. There is no final button you need to click to submit your paper; all the answers you'd saved will remain saved when the test ends.
    Clicking on the Leave Test button doesn't mean you won't be able to enter again.

  • Regarding MCQs and typed answers, you are expected to save frequently. Many of the buttons on the site involve refreshing the page and clicking on them will prompt you either to continue and discard your unsaved changes (if you have any), or to stay back and save your changes.

  • You should keep in mind that while this is a collaborative quiz, changes made by your teammates don't show up in realtime. If you're typing out an answer on a question, you need to be sure that none of your teammates are on the same question. If two people save answers for the same question back to back, the answer that's saved last will be retained while the other answer will be lost.
    So be sure to communicate with your teammates constantly.


  • You can mark a question for review, or as answered by clicking on these buttons.

    This changes the color of the question in the navigation bar for you and for your teammates the next time they refresh the page. Green for answered questions, and beige for questions marked for review.
    The purpose of marking questions is simply for you and your team to keep track of different questions, and it won't be seen by those who are correcting your paper.
    Note that these buttons cause the page to refresh, so be sure to save your answers before clicking on them.


  • As mentioned earlier, you can't see the changes made by your teammates in realtime. You have to refresh the page often to see new changes. This can also be done by clicking on the sync icons.



Format of the questions


There will be a total of four sections, one for each subject (Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Mathematics). All the questions (including the tie-breakers) are mandatory and contribute to the final score. The tie-breakers will be used to resolve ties, and hence the name. Each section will have 5 questions and will be graded out of 10 points. The maximum possible score that can be obtained overall in the Prelims round is 40 marks. The paper contains no negative marking. The split-up of the marks in each section is as follows:


  • Objective question - (1 mark)
    This question will either be a numerical question or a multiple-choice question with a single correct answer. There is no need to show working for this question and grading is automatic.

  • Oculomotor - (2 marks)
    This question will be in the form of a short video clip. Participants are supposed to look at the clip and then answer the question.

  • Trains of Thought - (2 marks)
    The question contains four possible lines of reasoning by which participants can reach an answer. Participants may choose one that they find reasonable and continue with it to arrive at the final answer. There may be more than one train of thought that leads to the right answer. Credit is only given for justification of the chosen reasoning option.
    Note: There may be more than 1 option that can lead to the right answer. The line of reasoning needs to lead to the answer, apart from being factually correct.

  • Subjective question - (2 marks)
    This question will be of the regular subjective kind, in which participants write out slightly detailed answers with justification.

  • Question with multiple sub-parts - (3 marks)
    This question will be divided into 3-5 easier sub-questions. Some of the sub-questions may rely on the answers from the previous sub-questions.
